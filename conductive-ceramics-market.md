<p><p>The conductive ceramics market comprises various types, each serving unique applications. Dielectric ceramics are insulating materials used in capacitors and insulators. Piezoelectric ceramics generate electric charge under mechanical stress, utilized in sensors and actuators. Ferroelectric ceramics exhibit spontaneous polarization, beneficial in memory devices and non-volatile storage. Magnetic ceramics, like ferrites, are crucial in electronics and magnetic applications. Other ceramics may include thermal conductors and superconductors, expanding the range of functionalities in industrial and technological sectors.</p></p>
